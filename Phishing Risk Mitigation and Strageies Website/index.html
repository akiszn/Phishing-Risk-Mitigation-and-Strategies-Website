What is
Phishing?

Phishing attacks, a significant threat to higher education institutions, compromise sensitive data, disrupt operations, and potentially lead to financial losses. Despite increasing awareness efforts, university personnel remain vulnerable to these attacks.

Phishing

Among cyber threats, phishing remains a leading attack method. It is a deceptive tactic used by cybercriminals to steal personal information. A cybercriminal creates a site by masquerading as a reputable source with an enticing request to lure the victim to trick them, just like how a fisherman uses a bait to catch a fish.



How does Phishing work?

Phishing works by luring a victim with legitimate-looking (but fraudulent) emails or other communication from a trusted (or sometimes seemingly desperate) sender who coaxes victims into providing confidential informationâ€”often on what looks to be a convincingly legitimate website. Sometimes malware or ransomware is also downloaded onto the victim's computer.



Are there any reported accidents of Phishing attacks in Higher Education?

Higher education institutions are increasingly targeted by cyberattacks, with phishing being a leading cause of data breaches that expose sensitive information and result in costly consequences (Fouad, 2021)[1]. For instance, a phishing attack at Lancaster University compromised the data of students, faculty, staff, and applicants, this highlights the vulnerability of academic institutions (Tripwire, 2019)[2]
In the Philippines, similar incidents have occurred, such as the breach at Romblon State University in 2024 and the University of the East in 2019, which exposed the personal data of students and alumni. Additionally, a vulnerability in the Philippine education ministry's system put over 210,000 records at risk (Dancel, 2024)[3], while Viettel Threat Intelligence (2024) reported over 315,000 compromised credentials across universities. These incidents highlight the urgent need for effective mitigation strategies to enhance phishing awareness and data security in educational institutions.


Are there any existing framework and principles regarding Phishing Awareness in PUPQC?

Despite growing awareness of cybersecurity threats, fewer efforts focus on human-centric strategies that address the behavioral and awareness gaps among end-users (Naqvi et al., 2023). This gap is particularly concerning for institutions like the Polytechnic University of the Philippines Quezon City (PUPQC), where personnel are highly vulnerable to phishing due to the absence of cybersecurity training programs, heavy reliance on email communication, and frequent use of digital tools and the Internet for data exchange.
This study examined the phishing awareness of PUPQC personnel, focusing on a human-centric approach to safeguard sensitive academic and personal data. It aimed in assessing their awareness levels and behavioral responses to phishing attempts, with the goal of developing a tailored phishing risk mitigation strategy that addresses the specific needs of PUPQC personnel.
